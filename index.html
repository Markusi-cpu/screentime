The Impact of Screen Time

Understanding how digital habits affect your well-being is the first step to creating balance.

Sleep Disruption

Blue light from screens can reduce melatonin production, making it harder to fall asleep.

71% of students report insufficient sleep

Mental Well-being

Excessive screen time is linked to increased anxiety and decreased concentration.

63% report increased stress from social media

Physical Health

From eye strain to reduced physical activity, screens can affect your body.

58% report eye strain and headaches

How Do You Compare?

The average teen spends over 7 hours daily on screens outside of school work.

Source: Common Sense Media Research, 2023

Practical Strategies

Simple habits that can make a big difference in your digital wellness.

Time Blocking

Schedule specific times for social media and entertainment. Use a timer to stick to your limits.

Learn more

Notification Detox

Turn off non-essential notifications. Check messages and updates at scheduled times.

Learn more

Screen-Free Zones

Create areas in your home where screens aren't allowed, like your bedroom or dining table.

Learn more

20-20-20 Rule

Every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds to reduce eye strain.

Learn more

Purposeful Browsing

Before opening an app, ask yourself why. Set a specific goal for your screen time.

Learn more

Social Accountability

Challenge friends to reduce screen time together. Share progress and support each other.

Learn more
